# Humanizer - 33 Patterns with Examples

## Content Patterns

### 1. Significance Inflation
- Before: "marking a pivotal moment in the evolution of..."
- After: "was established in 1989 to collect regional statistics"

### 2. Notability Name-dropping
- Before: "cited in NYT, BBC, FT, and The Hindu"
- After: "In a 2024 NYT interview, she argued..."

### 3. Superficial -ing Analyses
- Before: "symbolizing... reflecting... showcasing..."
- After: Remove or expand with actual sources

### 4. Promotional Language
- Before: "nestled within the breathtaking region"
- After: "is a town in the Gonder region"

### 5. Vague Attributions
- Before: "Experts believe it plays a crucial role"
- After: "according to a 2019 survey by..."

### 6. Formulaic Challenges
- Before: "Despite challenges... continues to thrive"
- After: Specific facts about actual challenges

## Language Patterns

### 7. AI Vocabulary
Replace: actually, additionally, testament, landscape, showcasing, pivotal, crucial, comprehensive, utilize, leverage, foster, facilitate, nuanced, intricate, robust, streamlined, cutting-edge, groundbreaking, innovative

### 8. Copula Avoidance
- Before: "serves as... features... boasts"
- After: "is... has"

### 9. Negative Parallelisms
- Before: "It's not just X, it's Y"
- After: State the point directly

### 10. Rule of Three
- Before: "innovation, inspiration, and insights"
- After: Use natural number of items

### 11. Synonym Cycling
- Before: "protagonist... main character... central figure... hero"
- After: "protagonist" (repeat when clearest)

### 12. False Ranges
- Before: "from the Big Bang to dark matter"
- After: List topics directly

### 13. Passive Voice
- Before: "No configuration file needed"
- After: Name the actor when it helps clarity

## Style Patterns

### 14. Em/En Dashes
- Before: "institutions—not the people—yet this continues—"
- After: Use periods, commas, colons, or parentheses

### 15. Boldface Overuse
- Before: "**OKRs**, **KPIs**, **BMC**"
- After: "OKRs, KPIs, BMC"

### 16. Inline-Header Lists
- Before: "**Performance:** Performance improved"
- After: Convert to prose

### 17. Title Case Headings
- Before: "Strategic Negotiations And Partnerships"
- After: "Strategic negotiations and partnerships"

### 18. Emojis
- Before: "🚀 Launch Phase: 💡 Key Insight:"
- After: Remove emojis

### 19. Curly Quotes
- Before: said "the project"
- After: said "the project" (straight quotes)

### 26. Hyphenated Word Pairs
- Before: "cross-functional, data-driven, client-facing"
- After: Drop hyphens on common word pairs

### 27. Persuasive Authority Tropes
- Before: "At its core, what matters is..."
- After: State the point directly

### 28. Signposting Announcements
- Before: "Let's dive in", "Here's what you need to know"
- After: Start with the content

### 29. Fragmented Headers
- Before: "## Performance" + "Speed matters."
- After: Let the heading do the work

### 30. Diff-Anchored Writing
- Before: "This function was added to replace..."
- After: Describe what it does, not what changed

### 31. Manufactured Punchlines
- Before: "It had no preference. No prior. No nostalgia."
- After: Use varied sentence lengths and concrete claims

### 32. Aphorism Formulas
- Before: "Symmetry is the language of trust"
- After: Replace with the actual claim

### 33. Conversational Rhetorical Openers
- Before: "Honestly? It depends..."
- After: Remove the fake-candid setup

## Communication Patterns

### 20. Chatbot Artifacts
- Before: "I hope this helps! Let me know if..."
- After: Remove entirely

### 21. Cutoff Disclaimers
- Before: "While details are limited in available sources..."
- After: Find sources or remove

### 22. Sycophantic Tone
- Before: "Great question! You're absolutely right!"
- After: Respond directly

## Filler and Hedging

### 23. Filler Phrases
- "In order to" → "To"
- "Due to the fact that" → "Because"
- "At this point in time" → "Now"
- "For the purpose of" → "To"
- "In the event that" → "If"

### 24. Excessive Hedging
- Before: "could potentially possibly"
- After: "may"

### 25. Generic Conclusions
- Before: "The future looks bright"
- After: Specific plans or facts
